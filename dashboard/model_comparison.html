UK Retail Dissolution — Model Comparison & Scenario Forecast

ARIMAX(2,1,1) + Holt ensemble · Backtested on rolling 12-month windows · 666,462 dissolved companies · Jan 2015–Jan 2026
Key methodological finding: Rolling backtests show Holt linear smoothing (OOS RMSE = 2,952) outperforms ARIMAX out-of-sample despite ARIMAX having better in-sample fit (RMSE = 1,998). This is the classic bias-variance tradeoff documented in Makridakis' M-competitions — simpler models generalise better on volatile short series. The final forecast uses a weighted ensemble (52% Holt + 48% ARIMAX), which minimises expected OOS error.

Why not Prophet or LSTM? Prophet is optimised for daily business data with strong weekly seasonality — it overfits the small seasonal component in monthly retail data (STL: 3.5%) and underestimates structural level shifts. LSTM requires far more observations (n ≥ 500+) and becomes a black box incompatible with causal inference. ARIMAX is the natural complement to the Granger/cointegration framework already in the paper: it uses the same structural relationship (online share → dissolution) and produces interpretable coefficients.

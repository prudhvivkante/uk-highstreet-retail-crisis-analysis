Four structural regimes visible: Pre-shock (Jan 2015–Feb 2020, avg 1,954/month) → Shock+moratorium (Mar 2020–Jun 2021, government halted dissolutions) → Rebound tsunami (Jul 2021–Dec 2022, pent-up closures released, 338% of pre-COVID rate) → New normal (2023–present, avg 9,917/month and still rising). Dashed grey = pre-COVID counterfactual trend; shaded gap = ~215,800 excess dissolutions.
Survival is collapsing by cohort. A retail company incorporated in 2010–2012 had a 76% chance of surviving 5 years. By 2016–2018 that had fallen to 4%. The 2019 cohort shows an anomalous uptick — the COVID moratorium prevented dissolutions, artificially inflating apparent survival. Note: curves truncate where the cohort has not yet reached that age; the 2019 and 2020–2021 curves drop steeply because those companies are only now reaching dissolution age in the post-moratorium surge.

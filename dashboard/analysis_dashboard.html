Online Share vs Dissolution (Structural Relationship)

Core finding: The UK retail sector has undergone a structural inversion. Before 2020 it generated +6,618 net new businesses/year (2016–2019). After 2022 this inverted to −1,648/year. January 2026 dissolution rate: 10,781/month = 5.9× pre-COVID average.
New finding — monthly panel: Granger causality at monthly frequency sharpens the causal lag from the previously estimated 24 months (annual aggregation artefact) to 1–2 months. Online share → dissolution at lag 1: F=25.26, p<0.001.

Data Quality Notes

Cardiff CF14 8LH: 1,892 default-address records excluded. 98.1% of apparent Cardiff records used CH's administrative default address — online-only retailers with no physical UK location.
Tuesday Batch Processing: 97.5% of dissolution dates fall on Tuesdays (CH admin batching). No economic meaning; monthly aggregation removes this artefact.
Rate-limited months: 8 months in 2016–2019 patched from manual downloads. June 2018 linearly interpolated. Feb 2025 is a lower bound (5,000-record cap).
